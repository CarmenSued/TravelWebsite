Places & Entertainment

Guzma's Castle

The Castle of Guzman the good is a medieval fortress built on the site of a Roman settlement. Work on the existing structure was begun in 960 by order of Caliph Abd al -Rahman III, who built the first enclosure on a trapezoidal plan. The castle is very well restored and has some multimedia exhibitions, great views of the city and the sea.

Tarifa's street

Tarifa is a special and enchanting town, the Moroccan influence mixed with the Andalusian style of white houses is present in their narrow streets being a perfect place to get lost and enjoy the very essence of this little town. On a clear day,there are several lookout points along the port to have coffee or tea while viewing the majestic views of the top of North Africa and enjoy the lights of tht side by night.

Punta Paloma

Punta Paloma is a little island connected to Tarifa in the southernmost point of Andalucia, it is an old military fort that is close to the public. Next to it, is one of the most amazing an unspoiled beach 10 minutes drive from Tarifa, kite-surfers have kept this place to themselves for years due to the wind, gold sand and crystal clear water and massives sand dunes created by the Atlantic wind.

Public Market

Start your day visiting the Public Market with touches of Moroccan architecture, the market has a lively morning that sells the catch of the day and products from locals farmers, then you can have tapas or lunch in the restaurants of the city or take a seat in a warm terrace by the beach in one of the typical chiringuitos along the cost.

Openening hours

Mon-Sat 9am to 3pm
